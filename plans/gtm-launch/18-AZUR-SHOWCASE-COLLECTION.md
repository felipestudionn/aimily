## Brand identity

### Name
**AZUR**

### Tagline
*Wear what the sea would wear.*

### One-liner
A women's resort-luxury label born on the Côte d'Azur. Sculpted linen, raffia, vegetable-tanned leather. Made in Provence, Portugal, and Mallorca.

### Brand DNA — long form
> AZUR is a Mediterranean luxury label for women who summer the same way every year — and never look the same twice. The collection lives on the line where Saint-Tropez at sunrise meets Cadaqués at noon: clean French architectural cuts, woven into Catalan and Andalusian artisanship. Colors are pulled directly from the coast — deep azure waters, bone-white walls, terracotta tile, olive groves at golden hour. The fabrics are 100% natural, mostly Italian linen and Egyptian cotton, never synthetic. Every piece is meant to be worn for five summers, not one.
>
> AZUR is for the woman who packs one suitcase for two weeks, who buys one beautiful dress and wears it to dinner with three different men over the course of a season, who reads Joan Didion on the deck of a boat and thinks her tan is the only accessory she needs. She is 28-42, urbane, has run her own business or is climbing the right one, and would rather spend €380 on a perfect linen dress than €120 on three forgettable ones.
>
> The brand is positioned in **contemporary luxury**: above Reformation and Rouje, peer-to-peer with Jacquemus and The Row's resort line, below Loro Piana and Hermès Plage. Price points anchor between €180 (a tank) and €490 (a structured dress).

### Tone of voice
- Sensorial. Salt, light, weight of fabric.
- Present tense. *"This is how summer feels."*
- Em-dashes welcome — they slow the reader down
- Lowercase: `azur`. Never AZUR or Azur.
- Sign-off: *"Côte du Sol."* — replaces "That's all." for AZUR-internal copy. (We keep "That's all." as aimily's signoff; AZUR has its own.)

### Visual identity
**Colors** (anchored on signature azure):
- **Azur** (signature) — `#1B4D8E` (deep Mediterranean blue)
- **Bone** — `#F5EFE6` (whitewashed wall)
- **Terre** — `#B85C3A` (Provençal terracotta)
- **Olive** — `#7A8F5F` (silver olive leaf)
- **Charbon** — `#1A1A1A` (kohl, eveningwear contrast)

**Typography**:
- Display: serif (think Times New Roman cut elegantly, or Editorial New)
- Body: clean sans (Inter, or Söhne)
- Brand mark: lowercase serif `azur` — single weight, tight tracking

**Logo direction**: simple wordmark `azur` in a fine serif on bone background. No icon. The brand IS the word.

## Customer profile (Consumer Definition block)

| Dimension | Value |
|---|---|
| **Primary persona** | "The Returning Summer Girl" — 32yo, Spanish/French/Italian/American, urban, single or partnered, no kids yet |
| **Income** | €60K-€150K/year personal, often higher household |
| **Locations** | Madrid, Barcelona, Paris, Milan, NYC, LA — with 2-3 weeks/year in Mallorca, Saint-Tropez, Capri, Tulum |
| **Wardrobe philosophy** | Buys 8-12 new pieces/year, keeps each 5-7 years, knows every garment in her closet |
| **Other brands she buys** | Jacquemus, Khaite, Toteme, La DoubleJ, Polo Ralph Lauren resort, Birkenstock, Hermès silk |
| **Reads** | Vogue Business, NYT Style, Apartmento, Vestoj, Cereal magazine |
| **Watches** | Aline Asmar d'Amman interviews, Loro Piana craftsmanship documentaries |
| **Decision driver** | Quality of fabric and cut — willing to pay 2x for "perfectly cut" over "trendy" |
| **Pain point** | Boutique shopping is expensive and slow; e-commerce is fast but loses craft narrative |

## Market research (Market Research block)

### Competitive landscape
| Brand | Price tier | Aesthetic distance | Why they're a peer/threat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Jacquemus** | €350-€800 | Direct peer | Same Mediterranean DNA, broader aesthetic |
| **Khaite** | €600-€1500 | Adjacent (NY-coded) | Same customer, more architectural |
| **Toteme** | €300-€700 | Adjacent (Scandi-clean) | Same minimalism, less warmth |
| **La DoubleJ** | €400-€1200 | Adjacent (Italian-romantic) | Same resort customer, more print-heavy |
| **Faithfull the Brand** | €150-€350 | Below (mass-resort) | Same vibe, accessible price |
| **Polo Ralph Lauren** | €200-€600 | Adjacent (American-coastal) | Same resort wear, less editorial |

### Trend signals (Spring/Summer 2027)
- **Quiet luxury permanence**: 4th season running, won't peak until 2028
- **Linen + raffia as the new logo**: "ostensible craft" replaces overt branding
- **Mediterranean anchor**: Tom Ford Beauty, Khaite, Frankies Bikinis all leaning Côte d'Azur
- **Anti-mass-resort backlash**: customers tired of "sundress" homogeneity, looking for cuts
- **Sustainable proof points**: customers care about supply chain transparency, not just claims

### White space
What competitors are NOT doing well:
- Truly Mediterranean *artisanal* (most brands in this space are NY/Stockholm — AZUR's Provence/Mallorca anchor is rare)
- Footwear at this tier with the same craft narrative (most brands punt footwear to wholesale partners)
- Color palette beyond bone+terracotta+navy — AZUR pushes olive and signature azure, broader aesthetic
