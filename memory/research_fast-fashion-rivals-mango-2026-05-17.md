## Executive summary — 10 hard facts

1. **Mango runs a TWO-vendor planning stack, not one.** Centric Software (PLM, since April 2013) + o9 Solutions (MFP / Assortment Planning / Demand Planning, announced 25 March 2025). The o9 deployment is "modernising fragmented, outdated systems" — Mango's own framing. **[VERIFIED]**
2. **Mango is a deliberately slower cadence than Zara.** Mango designs and produces its own collections rather than chasing the ultra-fast weekly cycle. Their Head of Womenswear Design Justicia Ruano publicly framed the strategic direction as "fashion that is a bit calmer, more timeless, more flexible, more quality, more durability." **[REPORTED]**
3. **Mango FY24 financials**: €3.339B turnover (+7.6% reported, +11.6% constant FX), gross margin 60.7%, EBITDA €636M (+19%), net profit €219M (+27%). 4E plan target = >€4B in 2026. **[VERIFIED — Mango press release]**
4. **Mango is in a governance window.** Founder Isak Andic died 14 December 2024 in a hiking accident. Toni Ruiz (CEO since 2020) became Chairman in January 2025. Helena Helmersson (ex-H&M CEO 2020-2024) joined Mango's board July 2025 as independent member. **[VERIFIED]**
5. **Mango steering committee = 11 people**, with Luis Casacuberta as Chief Product & Sustainability Officer (the executive whose remit includes seasonal review pain), Jordi Álex Moreno as CTO, Jochen Grosspietsch as Chief Supply Chain Officer, Margarita Salvans as CFO. Eva Gallego heads Mango Woman (reports to Casacuberta); Josep Estol heads Mango Man (reports to Casacuberta). **[VERIFIED]**
6. **Zara sell-through is reportedly >85% (vs industry 60-70%)**, with markdowns capped because the supply chain is twice-weekly and store-data driven. **[REPORTED — multiple trade sources, never disclosed by Inditex officially]**
7. **Shein's documented model**: 100-200 unit test orders, scaled up only if demand proves; <2% unsold inventory (industry avg ~30%); 2,000-10,000 new SKUs/day per Rest of World; supplier scorecards where bottom 30% of lowest tier are culled. **[REPORTED — Rest of World 2022 investigation, Bloomberg, Wired]**
8. **H&M 2018 disaster**: $4.3B unsold inventory equating to ~18% of sales; up to 47% markdowns deployed; CEO Karl-Johan Persson set target to reduce inventory to 12-14% of sales by 2019. **[VERIFIED — H&M earnings + NPR]**
9. **Asos crisis and recovery**: FY23 pretax loss £296.7M (vs £31.9M FY22), recovered FY24 by exiting unprofitable orders (+35% profit/order) and launching "Test & React" small-batch model (scaled to >10% of own-brand by end FY24, target 30%, 3-week design-to-site). **[VERIFIED — Asos FY23/FY24 reports]**
10. **McKinsey/BoF State of Fashion 2025 baseline**: 30-40% of all apparel sold at discount; $70-140B overproduction annually; up to 12% of fashion inventory unsold each season. These numbers establish the floor for any "we beat industry" claim. **[VERIFIED]**

### 4. Operating cadence — how often, how big, and what data

**[REPORTED] consensus from trade press**: Mango operates a more traditional 2-3 main season + capsule drop cadence (vs Zara's twice-weekly). Two annual main collections (SS, FW), with mid-season capsules and partner collections (Victoria Beckham anniversary capsule was 55 pieces, $80-$400 range; positioned as the model for future capsules).

This is REAL pain — slower cadence means decisions matter MORE (one wrong fall winter = whole season of margin destruction). Unlike Zara, Mango cannot rely on twice-weekly re-buys to fix a bad call. The forensic post-season is therefore more valuable to a Mango merchandising director than to a Zara one.

## Post-mortems: what KPIs did they miss?

### H&M 2018 — root cause analysis

**What they missed**: 9 weeks of unsold stock built up on the racks before the markdown reflex kicked in. Sourcing lead times of weeks-to-6-months meant they couldn't unwind the position by re-buying flexibly.
**KPIs that failed**: inventory weeks-on-hand was tracked but the *projected* weeks-on-hand at end-of-season was not actioned on aged-stock cohorts.
**What they changed**: faster sourcing, AI forecasting, RFID tracking, outlet expansion; explicit inventory-to-sales target (12-14%); collection simplification (Daniel Ervér 2024).
**Lesson for aimily**: a "projected weeks-on-hand at season-end" signal per SKU is the single most board-actionable KPI in a slow-cadence brand. Mango is precisely a slow-cadence brand.

Source: [BoF Inventory Case Study](https://www.businessoffashion.com/case-studies/news-analysis/hm-inventory-retail-supply-chain/), [Supply Chain Nuggets](https://supplychainnuggets.com/why-the-4b-hms-inventory-failure-happened/)

### Asos 2022-2024 — returns-driven margin collapse

**What they missed**: "Returns rate" was tracked but "profit per order net of returns and markdown" was not the primary lens. Top-line growth masked the unit-economics rot.
**KPIs that failed**: Customer-level profitability, SKU-level return-adjusted contribution.
**What they changed**: 
1. Exit of unprofitable orders/customers (+35% profit per order in FY24).
2. £1.99 returns fee.
3. Test & React small-batch model (>10% own-brand, target 30%, 3-week cycle, ~3 weeks of stock).
**Lesson for aimily**: return-rate-adjusted margin per SKU is now the table-stakes KPI for any e-com-heavy brand. Mango is 33% online — they need this lens whether they ask for it or not.

Source: [Asos FY24 Results](https://asos-12954-s3.s3.eu-west-2.amazonaws.com/files/6717/3078/6989/FY24_Results_Statement_041124_-_FINAL.pdf)

### Boohoo 2022-2023 — same crisis, less recovery

Boohoo took the same hit as Asos but with less of a recovery toolkit. Reduced inventory 36% but never developed an equivalent of Test & React; result is they're still loss-making.
**Lesson for aimily**: cutting inventory without a forensic per-SKU view is throwing the baby out with the bathwater. The "what to kill vs what to amplify" decision is what saves margin in a contraction.

## Shein's documented algorithm — verified vs speculation

**VERIFIED (multiple corroborating sources including supplier reporting and factory access)**
- Initial test batch ~100 units (some 50, some 200; the order-of-magnitude is rock-solid).
- 2,000-10,000 new SKUs added per day during the Rest of World monitoring window (Jul-Dec 2021).
- Supplier scorecard: 40% performance (timeliness/delivery/defects/new product success) + 60% order volume. Bottom 30% of lowest tier culled.
- Suppliers concentrated in Nancun Town, Guangzhou City.
- Algorithm scrapes social media + competitor sites + search queries + customer browsing/purchase history.

**REPORTED BUT UNAUDITED (treat with skepticism)**
- 98% sell-through / <2% unsold inventory. (Repeated across many sources but never with a financial-audit trail. Could be true, could be Shein-aligned PR.)
- 40-day inventory turnover. (Same skepticism.)
- "LATR" branding — Large-scale Automated Test and Reorder. (Name shows up in trade press but Shein has not published a methodology paper or whitepaper.)

**WHAT NO ONE HAS PUBLISHED**
- The exact reorder threshold (i.e., how many units of the 100 must sell in how many days to trigger a 1,000-unit reorder).
- The kill threshold for poor performers.
- The cap on the maximum reorder (does it stop at 10,000 or scale to 100,000?).

**Implication for aimily**: any threshold we set is more thoughtful than what Mango currently uses (PowerPoint + tribal memory). It does NOT need to match Shein's. It needs to be defensible against Mango's gross margin reality (60.7%) and their slower cadence.

## What this tells us about aimily In-Season's current thresholds

Our engine emits 9 verdicts. For each, we have implicit thresholds (the numbers Felipe acknowledges were "invented without backtest"). Below is the rival-operating-practice check — what the actual buyers in the market would consider supported vs contradicted vs unknown.

### 1. KILL (markdown to zero, exit SKU)

**Current implicit threshold**: SKU underperforms on velocity AND margin at end-of-window.
- **Supported by**: H&M post-2018 explicitly cut down stock with deep markdowns up to 47% within 9 weeks. Asos exits "least profitable orders" outright.
- **Contradicted by**: Inditex/Zara almost NEVER kill — they re-buy or replace twice weekly, so the kill signal is structurally different. Uniqlo doesn't kill (LifeWear basics carry over indefinitely).
- **Mango calibration**: Mango's slower cadence (2 main seasons) makes the kill decision binary and high-stakes. Threshold should be CONSERVATIVE — kill only if (return-adjusted sell-through < 30% at week 6) AND (effective margin < 30% after planned markdown) AND (no archetype recovery via color/size swap).
- **Verdict**: SUPPORTED in principle, needs Mango-specific calibration (60.7% gross margin = more headroom than Asos/Boohoo).

### 2. MARKDOWN

**Current implicit threshold**: Sell-through below target at midpoint, before full markdown season.
- **Supported by**: Zara markdowns at 4-6 weeks if velocity off pace (industry consensus). H&M 47% markdown depth post-2018. Most brands target 80% sell-through by markdown period start.
- **Mango calibration**: Mango's 60.7% gross margin means a 30-40% markdown is still margin-positive. Threshold can be more aggressive than Boohoo (~38% gross margin in 2022) or Asos (~43%).
- **Verdict**: SUPPORTED. Calibrate depth to brand's gross margin floor.

### 3. REPLENISH (N more units)

**Current implicit threshold**: SKU exceeds velocity threshold AND supply chain can deliver before season cliff.
- **Supported by**: Zara's whole model. Asos Test & React explicitly targets 3-week design-to-site. Shein's reorder loop.
- **Contradicted by**: Mango's slower sourcing footprint (Bangladesh/Vietnam/India = longer lead times). The "can supply chain deliver" gate is more critical for Mango than for Zara.
- **Mango calibration**: Replenish threshold must include a "weeks-to-supplier" check by sourcing country. Turkey/Italy nearshore = aggressive replenish OK. Vietnam/Bangladesh = must trigger ≥6 weeks before season cliff.
- **Verdict**: SUPPORTED for Turkey/Italy sourced; CONSTRAINED for Asia-sourced.

### 4. RESIZE_DOWN (reduce next buy)

**Current implicit threshold**: Lineage / style category showing declining sell-through across multiple SKUs.
- **Supported by**: H&M 2018 lesson — should have resized down before the inventory built up. McKinsey says 12% of fashion inventory unsold per season.
- **Mango calibration**: This is the most board-relevant verdict for Mango under their "calmer fashion" strategy. They are intentionally narrowing assortment.
- **Verdict**: STRONGLY SUPPORTED. Critical for the Mango pitch.

### 5. INVESTIGATE

**Current implicit threshold**: Signal anomaly — high returns + high sell-through, or unexpected color winner, or geo split divergence.
- **Supported by**: Nobody documents this verdict explicitly because it's by definition unautomated.
- **Mango calibration**: This is the verdict that protects the buyer's judgment — flagging "the data is weird, don't trust the auto-recommendation here." Critical for trust-building with senior merchants who otherwise distrust the tool.
- **Verdict**: SUPPORTED as a tool design principle, but threshold needs to be sensitive (better to flag too many than too few in pilot phase).

### 6. AMPLIFY_WINNER (replicate this style)

**Current implicit threshold**: SKU significantly outperforms lineage on margin AND velocity AND returns.
- **Supported by**: Shein LATR explicitly does this. Zara's "test 500 → reorder" model.
- **Contradicted by**: Mango's slower cadence means amplifying within-season is hard — most amplifies are "for next season."
- **Mango calibration**: Amplify verdict should produce TWO outputs: in-season (replenish + color extension) and next-season (lineage carryover into brief). The bifurcation matches the cadence reality.
- **Verdict**: SUPPORTED but split into in-season vs next-season actions per the audit note [AUDIT.md A.2 in this repo].

### 7. EXTEND_COLORS

**Current implicit threshold**: Style winning + specific color/size subsegment driving most of the win.
- **Supported by**: Standard buyer practice across all brands. Documented in Centric Software case studies.
- **Mango calibration**: Mango's color portfolio is wide (21 colors in their taxonomy per repo seed data). Extension verdict needs to be granular per archetype.
- **Verdict**: SUPPORTED. Already a strong differentiator vs o9 (which doesn't model color-within-style at this fidelity).

### 8. CARRYOVER

**Current implicit threshold**: SKU performs well, design still relevant, can extend into next season.
- **Supported by**: Uniqlo LifeWear is built around this. & Other Stories / COS premium positioning relies on it. Mango under Ruano's "calmer fashion" strategy is moving toward this.
- **Verdict**: STRONGLY SUPPORTED for Mango specifically. Likely the most underused verdict in current Mango practice.

### 9. HOLD

**Current implicit threshold**: SKU on plan, no action needed.
- **Supported by**: Self-evident, every brand does this implicitly by default.
- **Verdict**: SUPPORTED. The interesting design question is making sure HOLD is an EARNED state in the engine output (not just "we couldn't classify it") — otherwise buyers tune out.
