# Research · European fashion-retail business schools — operational craft taught in classroom

**Date**: 2026-05-17
**Purpose**: Ground aimily In-Season's per-SKU decision-support taxonomy in the operational vocabulary, decision flows, and frameworks actually taught to buyers/merchandisers in top European fashion-retail programs. Anchor for Mango pilot conversation (Barcelona, mid-market, just signed o9 Mar-2025 — IESE-based, Andic family historically tied to IESE).
**Method**: Public web research across program websites, official UAL/Bocconi/IESE pages, faculty bios, textbook publishers, industry-press operational write-ups (Style Arcade, First Friday, Retail Dogma).

## Executive summary — 10 hard findings

1. **The canonical artifact is the WSSI** (Weekly Sales, Stock & Intake, pronounced "wizzy"). It is the spine of every fashion-retail merchandising program in Europe — explicitly taught at LCF short course, embedded in MAFED's "Merchandising" + "Retail strategy & operations" modules, and is the literal definition of "operational merchandise control" in British curricula. **aimily In-Season must speak WSSI grammar** if we want a Mango buyer to feel native: Sales, Stock, Intake, OTB, cover weeks, sell-through, markdown.

2. **There is no MA Fashion Buying and Merchandising at LCF**. Felipe's brief assumed one; in fact LCF offers only **BA (Hons) Fashion Buying and Merchandising** (3-year undergrad, course leader **Zoe Hinton**) and **PG Cert Fashion: Buying and Merchandising** (15-week intensive, course leader **Helen Hindley**, ex-Head of Merchandising at House of Fraser). The PG Cert has applications suspended for 2026/27 — political signal worth tracking.

3. **Helen Hindley** (LCF PG Cert leader, ex-House of Fraser Head of Merchandising) is THE operationally credentialed lecturer in the UK academic system. Course faculty roster includes 5 senior practitioners — Nicole Dunlop (ex-M&S), Ginny Hutt (Arcadia/BHS/Tesco/Ralph Lauren, 30y), Stephanie Liberman (Debenhams/Very.co.uk/Sainsburys, 22y), Rob Chadband (merchandising/supply-chain 25y), Eddie Knevett (ex-Head of Fashion Accessories House of Fraser). This is the densest concentration of ex-buyer-now-faculty in Europe.

4. **Bocconi MAFED has a course literally titled "Buying processes in fashion"**, plus separate courses on Merchandising · Retail strategy & operations · Phygital strategy · Sales management — leadership: **Stefania Saviolo** (Director) + **Erica Corbellini** (faculty). Their textbook *Managing Fashion and Luxury Companies* (Etas, 2009; updated editions) is the European Italian-school canon — Chapter 12 (Saviolo) covers retail identity + distribution. Companion *L'esperienza del lusso* (2009) covers the luxury pyramid. MAFED page does NOT publish hour counts or per-course faculty maps — gap.

5. **IESE-Mango is a hard structural link**, not just a press flourish. **Mango Classroom inaugurated at IESE NYC campus on 2024-10-24** by Isak Andic + CEO Toni Ruiz. The case-study author is **Prof. Jordi Canals** (IESE Strategic Management, Fundación IESE Chair in Corporate Governance) — described as "personal friend of Andic, member of Mango board, author of the recent case study on the company." This is the IESE faculty member to namedrop in any aimily-Mango pitch. **Joan Jané** runs the NYC campus. **José Luis Nueno** (IESE Marketing) co-authored HBS case 9-703-497 "ZARA: Fast Fashion" with Pankaj Ghemawat — the most-taught Inditex case worldwide. **Frederic Sabriá** (IESE Operations) authored 15+ cases on logistics; Spain textiles commentator on Inditex/Mango.

6. **The weekly trading meeting is a Monday-morning ritual** taught uniformly across programs and industry training. Three pillars: (a) review trade performance vs KPIs, (b) tactical actions (replenish / markdown / chase / move), (c) team alignment on the week. **Backbone report = WSSI**. Sort hierarchy: total business → category → subcategory → SKU. **aimily's PDF overlay viewer is structurally correct** — it lives at the SKU sort tier where the buyer makes the call, after the funnel from total→cat→subcat narrows attention.

7. **The classroom decision thresholds are concrete and consistent with what aimily encodes**. From Style Arcade (industry-trained, used as supplemental at fashion programs): 50% sell-through in week 1 + 1 wk cover → **replenish for 8-week lifecycle** (Reponer N uds). 20+ weeks cover with 5 wks left in season → **Clear As You Go markdown** (Markdown). Size breaks <2 days = sizing ratio error; <2 weeks = quantity error (insufficient buy). Sales variance materially worse than stock variance (e.g., –15% sales / +25% stock) → **deep-dive analysis required** (Investigar). Zara hits ~85% full-price sell-through vs industry 60-70% — this is the operational benchmark students learn.

8. **The verb taxonomy of aimily In-Season maps cleanly onto classroom vocabulary** with one gap. Aimily verbs: Kill / Markdown / Reponer N uds / Reducir compra / Investigar / Replicar estilo / Extender colores / Mantener. Classroom equivalents: Kill = "drop"/"dog"/"clear out". Markdown = "markdown" / "Clear As You Go" / "rebajas" (ES). Reponer = "replenish" / "re-buy" / "reorder". Reducir compra = "cut OTB" / "reduce intake" / "phase back". Investigar = "deep-dive" / "exception report". Replicar = "carry-over winner" / "extend". Mantener = "core" / "continuity". **GAP: there is no clean classroom verb for "Extender colores con swatches del moodboard"** — this is aimily's creative-merch fusion, classroom teaches "extend the success" but not via creative-board color picking. That's aimily's wedge claim.

9. **Spanish operational vocabulary is more colloquial than the English canon** and matters for Mango. Key ES terms: *rebajas* (markdowns / sales period) · *saldo* (clearance) · *merma* (shrinkage, mismatch incl. overstock per McKinsey 2024 ~30% in sensitive cats) · *rotación* (rotation, turnover) · *presupuesto de compras* (open-to-buy). The Spanish operational dialect has no native equivalent for "WSSI" — it's used in English even in Spanish meetings. aimily UI in Spanish should use *rebajar / reponer / matar / reducir compra / investigar / replicar / extender / mantener*. Plain Castilian, not jargon — Felipe's instinct on output unit semantics matches the Mango merch director's daily speech.

10. **The IESE / Mango / o9 axis is the structural risk-AND-opportunity for aimily Strategy pitch**. Mango signed o9 in Mar 2025 (quant locked). IESE has the case study, the board friendship, the Mango Classroom NYC, and the executive education channel. **Pitch positioning**: aimily is the creative-merch overlay ABOVE the o9 quant plan, native to the IESE/Canals-taught case framework on Mango's family-business strategy. Frame in Spanish, frame as forensic per-SKU intelligence, frame as classroom-grade vocabulary. The Mango buyer is being trained at IESE and ISEM Madrid — both schools' alumni populate Mango's merch ladder.

## The canonical operational frameworks distilled

### A. The Weekly Trading Meeting ritual (Monday morning, universal across UK/EU programs)

**Attendees**: Department managers · buyers · merchandisers / planners (some sessions: marketing, ecom, allocation).

**Backbone report**: **WSSI** (Weekly Sales Stock & Intake) — pronounced "wizzy". Now extended to track margin + markdowns. Cost-WSSI vs Retail-WSSI distinction (taught at LCF short courses, referenced by First Friday).

**3 meeting purposes**:
1. Review trade performance vs KPIs — stay proactive, find opportunities
2. Decide tactical actions: replenishment · markdowns · stock moves · chase
3. Align the team on urgent tasks for the week ahead

**Sort hierarchy**: total business → category → subcategory → SKU. Multi-brand: brand layer interpolated above category.

**KPIs reviewed** (sequential, top to bottom):
- Sales performance: YTD vs MTD vs Last Week; revenue $ + units; variance analysis; channel split (ecom vs store); category/brand contribution; full-price vs markdown mix
- Product performance: **best sellers** ranked by $ / units / profit / sell-through %; **worst sellers** by low units / high stock $ / weak sell-through; size availability; weeks-in-business duration
- Stock management: stock-to-sales variance; intake received + on-order; **weeks of cover** (stock ÷ weekly sales)
- Aged inventory: 10-12 months in stock flagged (the "dogs" tier)

**Decision triggers (the canonical thresholds taught)**:

| Trigger | Action verb |
|---|---|
| 50% sell-through in week 1 + 1 wk cover | **Replenish for 8-week lifecycle** |
| Category 20+ wks cover, 5 wks before season end | **Clear As You Go markdown** |
| Size breaks <2 days from launch | Sizing ratio error → **fix mix, re-allocate** |
| Size breaks <2 weeks from launch | Insufficient quantity bought → **re-buy or accept** |
| Sales variance materially worse than stock variance (e.g. –15% / +25%) | **Deep-dive analysis** required |
| Aged stock 10-12 months | **Markdown / clearance / kill** |
| Item entered season as "dog" (no traction wk 1-3) | **Move to clearance, free OTB** |
| Best seller, strong sell-through, lead time fits remaining season | **Re-buy / chase / replicate** |
| Core item (continuity, basics) | **Maintain 100% size availability always** |

**Pre-meeting prep**: the "Monday morning scramble" — merch teams manually pull data + format reports. Pain point that Style Arcade, BlueYonder, Toolio, o9 (Mango), and aimily In-Season all monetize.

### B. The buyer's weekly decision flow (from RNK / sales report → action)

1. **Open the RNK / WSSI** for the week just closed.
2. **Filter to attention zone**: top 20 best sellers + bottom 20 worst sellers + everything with size breaks or variance flags.
3. **For each best seller**: ask — does sell-through justify re-buy? Does lead time fit remaining season? Is there cannibalisation risk on a related style? → **Reponer / Replicar / Extender**.
4. **For each worst seller**: ask — is it priced right? Is it sized right? Is it positioned right (store / page / ranges)? Is there a markdown that catches demand or do we kill? → **Markdown / Kill / Investigar**.
5. **For variance flags** (sales-vs-stock mismatch): deep-dive. Could be channel shift, weather, marketing failure, allocation error. → **Investigar**.
6. **Cross-check OTB**: do I have budget to act on the re-buys? Do I need to phase back current orders to free cash? → **Reducir compra**.
7. **Build the action list** for the Monday meeting + the buyer's note to the planner.
8. **Communicate** to allocation/store ops/ecom: what moves, what marks down, what stays.

### C. Re-buy / kill / markdown classroom decision triggers

- **Re-buy (replenish)** trigger: best-seller status (top decile by sell-through or top quintile by margin contribution) + lead time fitting at minimum half the remaining season + OTB available + no end-of-season inventory glut threat.
- **Kill (clear out)** trigger: dog (bottom decile sell-through after 3+ weeks at full price) + no markdown will recover margin + space cost > carrying value → free OTB.
- **Markdown** trigger: 20+ weeks of cover with 5 weeks of season left; or aged 10-12 months in stock; or competitive markdown environment (when peers mark down, you risk being left holding inventory). Depth depends on remaining season + competitor depth + brand positioning. Zara's 85% full-price sell-through (vs industry 60-70%) is the gold-standard benchmark.
- **Maintain (core/continuity)** trigger: basics with stable velocity year-round → maintain 100% size availability always; never markdown.

### D. Cross-functional decision boundary
- **Buying owns**: range / SKU mix / price architecture / colour & size assortment / vendor selection.
- **Merchandising owns**: stock by location / WSSI / OTB / markdown cadence / weeks of cover.
- **Planning owns**: total OTB budget / financial reconciliation / forecasts.
- **Sales / ops owns**: store-level execution, visual merchandising, training.
- **The conflict**: buyer wants to re-buy a hot style; merchandiser worries OTB is tight; planner enforces the financial frame. Aimily In-Season sits at the buyer-merchandiser intersection — must surface OTB impact alongside SKU decisions.

## What this tells us about aimily In-Season's taxonomy

**Strong alignment (no changes needed)**:
- Reponer N uds = Replenish — direct match, native to WSSI grammar.
- Kill = Drop / dog removal — direct match, classroom standard.
- Markdown = Rebajar / Clear As You Go — direct match.
- Reducir compra = Cut OTB / phase back — direct match.
- Investigar = Exception deep-dive — direct match.
- Mantener = Continuity / core — direct match.

**Partial alignment (slight gap to communicate)**:
- Replicar estilo — classroom says "carry-over winner" but aimily includes color/material/silhouette adjacencies. Need a one-liner that frames it as "carry-over with intelligent variation".
- Reducir compra — classroom phrasing is "phase back" or "cancel intake"; the Spanish *Reducir compra* is plain enough but consider adding "(cancelar / fasear)" in tooltips.

**Aimily's genuine new ground**:
- **Extender colores con swatches del moodboard** — there is NO classroom equivalent. Classroom teaches "extend the success" but never connects it to the creative direction artifact (moodboard / color palette). This is aimily's creative-merch fusion claim and where the pitch story is strongest.

**Recommendations**:
1. In aimily In-Season UI, add EN/ES toggle that uses the operational vocabulary table above verbatim. Mango buyers will switch between languages — the system speaking their classroom dialect builds trust fast.
2. Use *Reponer / Matar / Rebajar / Reducir compra / Investigar / Replicar / Extender / Mantener* for the Spanish UI. Avoid Anglicisms except WSSI/OTB/sell-through which Spanish operations teams use in English anyway.
3. The rationale prose attached to each action should reference the canonical decision threshold (e.g., "50% sell-through wk 1 + cover ≤1 wk → re-buy fits 8-week lifecycle") — this matches what an LCF PG Cert grad or ISEM Master FBA grad expects to read.
4. In the Mango pitch, anchor on Jordi Canals' case-study framework (Mango as Andic-family strategic asset under executive transition) — aimily as the SKU-level operating overlay that supports the new generation's executive transition with senior-buyer-grade decision support.
5. Consider a sponsored case-study collaboration with IESE (Canals/Nueno/Sabriá axis) or ISEM Madrid — "aimily × Mango × IESE: per-SKU forensic intelligence in classroom" is a credible artifact that builds enterprise trust.
