## 3 · The universal in-season ritual reconstructed

Cross-source consensus on the actual Monday Trade Meeting:

### Cadence
- **Frequency**: weekly, Monday morning. Universally.
- **Duration**: 90 minutes (Style Arcade + Commerce Thinking, documented).
- **Attendees**: 8-15 people. Typically: Head of Buying, Head of Merchandising, Senior Buyer per category, Senior Merchandiser per category, CFO rep (in some setups), Director of Sales/Store Ops (sometimes).

### Agenda structure (Style Arcade + Commerce Thinking deep dive)
1. Recap of last week's trade.
2. Headline KPIs — sales vs plan (week, MTD, YTD), GMROI, sell-through vs plan curve, stock vs intake.
3. **Category review** (the meat — top 10 best + top 10 worst per category).
4. Special situations (new launches, marketing campaigns coming, returns issues).
5. Open-to-buy / WSSI projection — where's the budget at, what re-buys are coming, what's at risk.
6. **Decisions log** — for each flagged SKU/cluster: action assigned, owner, deadline.

### Artifacts reviewed
- The WSSI itself (Weekly Sales Stock & Intake).
- The Six-Month Plan progress sheet.
- The Open-to-Buy worksheet.
- Best-sellers and worst-sellers reports (often top/bottom 10 or 20 per category).
- Stock-by-store / stock-by-size grid (for size break detection).
- Returns dashboard (where present).

### Decisions emitted (the verb set the discipline already has — see §5)
Reorder, Reorder+Distort, Markdown (initial/second/terminal), Cancel/Reduce on Order, Transfer between doors, Promote/Push, Maintain, Investigate, Discontinue, Pull forward intake, Push allocation.

### The Mango context
Mango runs WSSI internally (Spanglish — "wizzy"). They are a fashion retailer ~£3.3B revenue, 2,900 stores, 120 markets. The Monday Trade Meeting almost certainly happens at HQ in Palau-Solità i Plegamans for each category. **The pain we automate**: a single buyer at Mango can't run forensic SKU-level analysis across 18,000+ garments/year. They run headline analysis in the Monday meeting and trust their gut on the 80% they don't have time for. **That 80% is where the €33M of inventory leakage lives** (= 100bps of revenue, the threshold for Margarita Salvans' ROI math).

### Verbs aimily MISSING that the canon has (P1 add-list — Felipe scope decision applied 2026-05-17)

**Scope decision**: inter-store TRANSFER is OUT (allocator / operations layer). AMPLIFY_DISTRIBUTION (warehouse → more stores) is IN (buyer-merchandiser layer). See [`feedback_aimily-scope-no-inter-store-transfer.md`](../../../.claude/projects/-Users-felipemartinez-aimily/memory/feedback_aimily-scope-no-inter-store-transfer.md).

| Missing verb | Classroom name | Spanish | What it does | Why we need it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AMPLIFY_DISTRIBUTION | Extend distribution / Roll out to more doors | Ampliar distribución | Push warehouse stock to MORE stores (broaden coverage) when SKU is selling well in stocked stores and we have warehouse units | Commercial decision in buyer-merch layer. Caro & Gallien 2010 documents this exact mechanic in Zara's shipment optimization. |
| PROMOTE / PUSH | Promote / Push | Promocionar / Empujar | Velocity low but cause is KNOWN (upcoming marketing, campaign, weather) — give it marketing/visibility lever instead of writing it off | Without this, INVESTIGATE swallows two distinct decisions (cause unknown vs cause known + actionable). |
| PULL FORWARD INTAKE | Pull forward / Accelerate intake | Acelerar entrada | Move PO arrival date earlier when demand surprises upward | Standard operational verb when a hero is ramping faster than expected. We don't have it. |
| ~~TRANSFER between stores~~ | ~~Transfer / Inter-store transfer~~ | ~~Transferir~~ | ~~Move stock from low-velocity store to high-velocity store~~ | **OUT OF SCOPE** — allocator / operations layer, not buyer-merchandiser. Mango's logistics + o9 handle this. We compete in the commercial decision layer, not operations. |
| ~~PUSH ALLOCATION~~ | ~~Push allocation / Re-allocate~~ | ~~Reasignar~~ | ~~Redistribute existing in-network stock between stores~~ | **OUT OF SCOPE** — same as TRANSFER. The warehouse → more stores variant is AMPLIFY_DISTRIBUTION (above). Store-to-store rebalancing is allocator territory. |

### Verbs aimily has that should be SPLIT

1. **AMPLIFY_WINNER** should split into:
   - `amplify_in_season` = "Reorder + Distort NOW" (in-season action — buy more, bias toward winning stores/sizes/colors)
   - `amplify_next_season` = "Feed next-season's design brief" (creative-direction action — design 2-3 sequels)
   
   The classroom teaches these as separate decisions. They have different owners (buyer for in-season; design + buyer for next-season) and different timelines (this week vs Q4 planning).

2. **INVESTIGATE** should split into:
   - `investigate_root_cause` = "Cause unknown — review fit/quality/sizing/pricing"
   - `promote_push` = "Cause known — apply marketing/visibility lever"
   
   Currently we conflate both. Splitting clarifies the action for the buyer.

### Verbs aimily has that should be ENRICHED

3. **REPLENISH** should carry "distort" metadata:
   - Current: "Reponer 320 unidades"
   - Enriched: "Reponer 320 unidades · **distort hacia tiendas top-3 (Madrid Goya, Barcelona Diagonal, Bilbao Gran Vía) · tallas top-3 (M/L/S) · color 401**"
   - That's the operational verb "Reorder + Distort" that the classroom teaches.

4. **MARKDOWN_ACCELERATE** should carry the multi-step ladder explicitly:
   - Current: "Rebajar — 50% off"
   - Enriched: "Rebajar **Initial -25%** (semana 1) → **Second -40%** (semana 4) → **Terminal -70%** (semana 7+)"
   - This matches Donnellan ch.12 verbatim and the universal markdown cadence taught across LCF/FIT/LIM/FRA.

### Resulting target taxonomy (Round 2 + Felipe scope decision)

**13 verbs** (was 9, target was 14, settled at 13 after Felipe excluded inter-store transfer):

1. KILL (Discontinue)
2. MARKDOWN_ACCELERATE (multi-step ladder -25/-30 → -40/-60 → -70, 3-week steps)
3. REPLENISH (with distort metadata: top stores/sizes/colors)
4. **AMPLIFY_DISTRIBUTION** (NEW — warehouse → more stores, buyer-merch layer)
5. **PULL_FORWARD_INTAKE** (NEW — accelerate PO arrival)
6. RESIZE_DOWN
7. **PROMOTE_PUSH** (NEW — split from INVESTIGATE; marketing lever when cause known)
8. INVESTIGATE_ROOT_CAUSE
9. AMPLIFY_IN_SEASON (split from AMPLIFY_WINNER — Reorder + Distort NOW)
10. AMPLIFY_NEXT_SEASON (split from AMPLIFY_WINNER — sequel design brief)
11. EXTEND_COLORS (with moodboard swatches — the defensible wedge)
12. CARRYOVER
13. HOLD

The order above matches operational urgency. KILL / MARKDOWN / AMPLIFY_DISTRIBUTION are this-week decisions. PULL_FORWARD / REPLENISH / RESIZE_DOWN / PROMOTE / INVESTIGATE / AMPLIFY are this-month. EXTEND_COLORS / CARRYOVER / AMPLIFY_NEXT_SEASON feed creative direction next season.

**Explicitly OUT of scope**: inter-store TRANSFER and PUSH_ALLOCATION (store-to-store rebalancing). Those are allocator / operations layer — Mango logistics + o9 handle them. We compete in the commercial decision layer.

## 6 · The 4 books that actually matter

Agent C's ranking was decisive — these 4 books cover the entire operational craft and should be ordered, read, and cited:

### 1. Goworek, Helen — "Fashion Buying" (Wiley, 2nd ed)
- **Author background**: Leeds Beckett University, ex-buyer
- **Core contribution**: the canonical buying-cycle structure. The 8-stage buying cycle (range planning → comparative shop → sourcing → sample → critical path → negotiate → place order → review) is in every UK fashion-business curriculum.
- **Operational chapters**: Ch.4 Range Planning, Ch.6 Sourcing, Ch.8 Critical Path & Negotiation, Ch.10 In-Season Management.
- **Use for aimily**: the rationale prose for AMPLIFY / INVESTIGATE / KILL should reference the buying-cycle stage (where in the 8 stages are we?).

### 2. Jackson, Tim & Shaw, David — "Mastering Fashion Buying and Merchandising Management" (Palgrave)
- **Author backgrounds**: LCF, ex-M&S and ex-Burton respectively
- **Core contribution**: THE canonical European text on the **buyer vs merchandiser discipline boundary**. Who decides what.
- **Operational chapters**: Ch.3 The Buyer's Role, Ch.4 The Merchandiser's Role, Ch.7 Working as a Team, Ch.10 In-Season Management.
- **Use for aimily**: the verdict owner mapping (who acts on each verb). KILL → buyer + merchandiser jointly. MARKDOWN → merchandiser primary. REPLENISH → merchandiser primary, buyer signs off. TRANSFER → allocation team. PROMOTE → buyer + marketing.

### 3. Shaw, David & Koumbis, Dimitri — "Fashion Buying: From Trend Forecasting to Shop Floor"
- **Authors**: LIM College
- **Core contribution**: the "instinct vs planning" framing — when does the buyer override the data, when do they defer to it.
- **Operational chapters**: Ch.5 Sales Forecasting, Ch.7 Buying Decisions, Ch.9 In-Season Adjustments.
- **Use for aimily**: the confidence dimension — when our verdict should DEFER to the buyer's gut (low confidence_demand or confidence_creative_fit), and when it should LEAD (high confidence_action across all 6 dimensions).

### 4. Kincade, Doris & Gibson, Fay Y. — "Merchandising of Fashion Products" (Pearson, Virginia Tech)
- **Authors**: Virginia Tech faculty
- **Core contribution**: the US-side **Six Rights** (right product, right price, right place, right time, right quantity, right promotion) + model-stock-budget math.
- **Operational chapters**: Ch.4 The Six Rights, Ch.8 Model Stock Plan, Ch.11 Open-to-Buy Mechanics, Ch.13 Markdown Planning.
- **Use for aimily**: the Six Rights as a verdict rationale framework. Each action verb explicitly tied to which "Right" is being optimized. E.g., MARKDOWN = right price + right time; TRANSFER = right place; REPLENISH = right quantity + right time; PROMOTE = right promotion.

### Bonus: Angella Hoffman "Fundamentals of Merchandising Math and Retail Buying"
- The book with chapters literally titled "Monday Morning Reports: Sales" and "Monday Morning Reports: Markdowns". Operational treasure.
- Use for: validating UI copy + the Daily Trading Meeting copilot (Zara canonical name; cadencia per-tenant: Zara daily, Mango weekly Monday) framing.

### Strategy-tier (for Mango pitch credibility, NOT for verb design)

- **Saviolo, Stefania & Testa, Salvo** — "Strategic Management in the Fashion Companies" (Bocconi)
- **Corbellini, Erica & Saviolo, Stefania** — "Managing Fashion and Luxury Companies" (Bocconi, also a free Coursera MOOC — useful to drop in pitch as "we've internalized this framework")
- **Godart, Frédéric** — "Unveiling Fashion" (INSEAD)
- **Robin Lewis** — "The New Rules of Retail" (US strategy)

## 9 · Headline KPIs to surface in the UI

Agent B identified the gap: aimily has 6-dim confidence (ML-grade) but doesn't surface the buyer's expected headline KPIs. Adding them to the top row closes the trust gap.

### The 5 KPIs the buyer expects to see first

1. **GMROI** — Gross Margin Return On Inventory = (Gross Margin $) / (Avg Inventory at Cost)
   - **Target**: 3.0-3.5 for fashion (Kincade & Gibson Ch.7).
   - **What it tells the buyer**: how much margin each euro of inventory generates per year.
   - **Where to surface**: per-category dashboard + per-SKU detail row.

2. **Sell-through % vs week-of-season plan curve** (not raw STR)
   - **What**: actual sell-through plotted against the planned curve for this week of season.
   - **Why**: a 30% STR at week 4 means very different things if plan is 20% (hero ahead of plan) vs plan is 50% (dog behind plan).
   - **Where to surface**: per-SKU detail row, line chart.

3. **Weeks of cover vs lead time ratio**
   - **What**: WOC / supplier lead time.
   - **Why**: WOC of 8 weeks is dangerous if lead time is 10 weeks; safe if lead time is 4 weeks.
   - **Where**: per-SKU verdict card, single number.

4. **Stock-to-sales ratio**
   - **What**: stock value / weekly sales value.
   - **Why**: classical retail planning anchor. Spike = parked stock.
   - **Where**: per-category dashboard.

5. **Maintained markup %**
   - **What**: (cumulative margin after markdowns) / cumulative sales.
   - **Why**: tells the buyer how much margin they're actually keeping vs the initial markup.
   - **Where**: per-SKU detail row + per-category dashboard.

### UI hierarchy proposal

```
Top row (Monday-morning trading view, per category)
├── Headline KPIs: GMROI · ST vs plan · FWOC vs lead time · S/S ratio · Maintained markup
├── Top 10 bestsellers + Top 10 worst-sellers (the classroom canonical sort)
└── New launches this week (< 14 days in store)

Middle row (per-SKU verdict pack)
├── Verdict stack (1-N actions from the 12-verb taxonomy)

├── Per-store breakdown (for TRANSFER decisions)

```
