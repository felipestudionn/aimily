## What "creating a fashion collection" actually means

A fashion collection is not a logo and a vibe. It's a structured set of products with a coherent point of view, priced to a defined consumer, produced to a deadline, and launched into specific channels. To ship one, four things have to happen — and they have to happen in order, with data flowing forward at each step:

1. **Brand definition** — heritage, voice, values, references, consumer profile, palette. This is what every other decision reads from.
2. **Range planning** — which products at which prices, allocated to which channels, costed against factory MOQs.
3. **Technical development** — sketches, BOM, colorways, measurements, construction, samples, approvals.
4. **Launch coordination** — editorial imagery, drop calendar, content per SKU per channel, sales tracking.

Most brands handle each step in a different tool. The brand brief lives in Notion, the range plan lives in Excel, the tech pack lives in a separate PDF, the marketing calendar lives in Google Calendar, the samples are tracked over WhatsApp. Nothing reads from anything. Each handoff loses information.

aimily is the only platform that handles all four steps in one continuous workflow, with data flowing forward block-to-block. That's why a small team using aimily can ship a collection in 6 weeks that would take 4-8 months in the traditional stack.

## The 6-week timeline

Honest target for a focused 1-3 person team starting from scratch (no existing brand, no archive, no factory relationship):

**Week 1 — Brand DNA**. Write a one-paragraph brief. Run the Brand DNA model in aimily. Edit fields. Lock. Output: a structured brand definition (heritage, voice, values, references, consumer profile, palette) that every downstream block reads from.

**Week 2 — Range plan**. From the locked DNA, generate the range plan. 24 SKUs across 4 families is the typical first-collection scope. Pricing tier inherits from the consumer profile. Channel allocation derives from the same. Reconcile budget against factory MOQs (the materials library includes vendor MOQ + lead time data). Sign with finance (or with yourself, if you're the founder).

**Weeks 3-4 — Tech packs**. Each SKU opens in the design block. Add sketches (manually or AI-generated from a reference photo). Apply the brand palette via the Pantone TCX picker. Build the BOM from the 963-entry materials library — fabrics, hardware, linings, certifications. Set construction details and measurements. AI Margin Protection flags any SKU below your target margin and proposes substitutions.

**Week 5 — Approval and factory**. Multi-stage approval with email notifications. Export PDF tech pack in 3-5 seconds (Centric and PTC FlexPLM users wait minutes). Send to factory via Vendor Portal token URL. Receive quote, negotiate, place sample order.

**Week 6 — Samples + launch prep**. Sample tracking chain logs every iteration. AI photo comparison diffs the received sample against the original sketch. While samples are in production, the marketing block generates editorial imagery (no shooting needed), drop calendar coordinated across stockists, and content per SKU per channel. The launch is set up before the samples even land.

Compare to the traditional timeline: 6 months for the same work, with three sample rounds, two missed deadlines, and a launch pushed to the next season.

## What you need to start

- **A point of view**. aimily's Brand DNA module gives you structure, not taste. You need to know roughly what kind of brand you want to make.
- **A budget**. For a 12-24 SKU first collection in small-batch production (Portugal, Spain, Italy, Romania, Eastern Europe), realistic budget is €15-50k for samples + first run depending on materials and complexity. aimily helps you reconcile budget vs MOQ before you spend.
- **A factory partner** — either one you already know, or one from aimily's Vendor Portal directory. Most factories take a tech pack PDF as input and return a sample.
- **An aimily account**. Starter at €159/month is enough for a solo founder. Professional at €479/month adds the video reel generation and priority email support that's helpful when you're shipping multiple drops.

## When NOT to use aimily

Honesty matters in this business. aimily is the wrong choice if:

- You're a 500+ person enterprise running multi-region production with deep ERP integration. Centric Software or PTC FlexPLM fit you better.
- You only need a 3D design tool (CLO3D, Browzwear) and have no PLM/marketing requirements. Don't buy a four-block platform for one block.
- You're not in fashion. aimily is fashion-specific; it works for accessories, jewelry, footwear and adjacent categories but not for non-fashion verticals.

For everyone else — solo founders, 2-50 person teams, brands that want to ship a collection this season instead of in 18 months — aimily is the right tool.
