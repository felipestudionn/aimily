## What Centric Software is, honestly

Centric Software is the dominant fashion PLM in the world. Headquartered in Campbell, California, founded 2003, acquired by Dassault Systèmes (no — by Aptos in 2024). It serves around 800 fashion, footwear and beauty brands, including most of the names you recognize at the luxury and contemporary tier. Their Adobe Illustrator plugin is the deepest in the PLM market. Their Visual Boards module is the slickest executive-review surface in the industry.

Centric's strength is breadth and reference list. If you're a 500-person team running multi-region production with three years of customizations baked into your Centric instance, this comparison page isn't for you — stay where you are.

## What aimily is

aimily is the only AI-native, end-to-end fashion platform. Built by StudioNN, a fashion agency consulting independent fashion brands across Madrid, Barcelona, Milan and Paris since 2023. The product covers four blocks in one continuous data flow:

- **Block 1 · Brand DNA** — a 60-minute brief-to-DNA generation, with consumer profile, palette extraction (Pantone TCX 2,317 with ΔE2000), reference set.
- **Block 2 · Range plan** — CFO-ready in one day. Pricing tier inherited from Block 1, channel split derived from consumer profile, AI Margin Protection that suggests material substitutions.
- **Block 3 · Design & Development** — PLM-equivalent. Tech pack PDF in 3–5 seconds, materials library 963 verified B2B-supplier entries, multi-stage approvals, Compliance Hub + Vendor Portal, Construction Details, Artworks Library, multi-currency ECB, PO variance, multi-view drawings, sample tracking with AI photo comparison.
- **Block 4 · Marketing & Launch** — AI editorials on-model without photoshoot, drop calendar with cross-block dependencies, content studio per SKU per channel, live sales dashboard.

PLM-Parity Phase 1-7 closed on 3-4 May 2026. Documented in `~/aimily/.research/plm-design-development-deep-dive.md` (a 15-feature × Centric + PTC FlexPLM analysis with vendor + G2 + Capterra + SelectHub citations).

## Where Centric wins

- **Reference list and scale**: 800+ brands deployed, including most of the names you recognize. aimily launched into production in 2026 and is not at this scale.
- **Adobe Illustrator integration depth**: their Adobe Connect plugin (released 2016, refined annually) is the deepest PLM-Illustrator integration in the market. Designers who live in Illustrator will find Centric's plugin more mature than aimily's web-based sketch flow.
- **Visual Boards**: a touchscreen-first executive-review surface optimized for boardroom presentations. aimily doesn't have a direct equivalent.
- **Centric AI Fashion Inspiration**: their 2024 GenAI release for ideation. Beautiful imagery, trained on 1B+ images. Useful as an inspiration tool — though not a tech-flat generator, and it doesn't read from a brand DNA.
- **Mobile sample review app**: photograph the sample, drop a pin, comment in real time during fit sessions. aimily's web equivalent works on tablet but doesn't have a dedicated mobile app yet.

## Where aimily wins

- **Block 1, 2, 4 coverage**: Centric stops at the tech pack. aimily covers brand DNA, range plan and marketing/launch in the same platform with data flowing forward block-to-block. The right comparison isn't "aimily vs Centric for tech packs" — it's "aimily vs Centric + InDesign + Excel + Notion + a marketing agency".
- **Tech pack PDF speed**: 3–5 seconds vs minutes. Centric's PDF render time is the most-cited pain point in their G2 reviews. aimily renders 30-page A3 PDFs via Puppeteer + lightweight Chromium on Vercel Fluid Compute — the modern serverless stack rather than the legacy multi-page server-side template engine.
- **Pantone hex → TCX closest match (ΔE2000)**: Centric's library lets you browse and pick by code; aimily lets you paste any HEX and get the top three closest Pantones. The bridge unlocks 3D rendering tools (CLO3D, Browzwear) that work in HEX but require Pantone for production.
- **AI photo comparison sample vs sketch**: unique to aimily. Snap a photo of the sample, the AI compares it to the original sketch, flags discrepancies. Centric has sample-review workflow but no AI diff.
- **Multi-pin annotations on tech pack drawings**: aimily ships Hatch-style anchored comments with threaded discussion. Centric has flat object-level comments. This matters when a tech designer needs to mark "sleeve 1cm too long" specifically on the cuff in the front view of revision 4.
- **AI in every block, not in one module**: 16 AI endpoints in a connected data flow. Centric AI Fashion Inspiration is one tool; aimily's AI is in brand-DNA generation, range-plan suggestions, sketch-to-tech-pack extraction, AI Margin Protection, sample comparison, editorial generation, caption writing — all reading from the same brand DNA.
- **Pricing**: Starter €159/month, Professional €479/month, Pro Max €1,199/month, Enterprise from €3,000/month. Centric's pricing is private, but credible market sources put a typical small-mid Centric deployment at €40K–80K/year plus customization fees. The commonly cited Capterra complaint: "Many times you don't realize you're missing something until you use it. Once you go live anything you need unlocked or editable costs extra."
- **Time to value**: aimily's free trial → first collection in 90 seconds. Centric implementations average 6–12 months.

## Honest decision tree

**Choose Centric if**: you're a 500-person enterprise with multi-region production, you have an existing Adobe Illustrator design culture nobody is willing to disrupt, you've already paid for Centric and your customizations are deep, your CIO has standardized on Dassault adjacent tooling.

**Choose aimily if**: you're a 1-50 person fashion brand, your real cost today is the *fragmented stack* (PLM doesn't exist, design lives in Excel, brand brief lives in Notion, marketing scrambles two weeks before launch), you need AI in every block not in one module, you can't deploy 6-12 months of integration work, you'd rather pay €159/month than €40K/year.

**Run both if**: you're scaling from 50 to 200 people and not ready to commit. aimily covers the day-to-day; Centric becomes the system-of-record once volume justifies it. Several teams do this.

## How to migrate

If you're moving from Centric to aimily:

1. **Export tech packs as PDF** from Centric — the universal format every fashion factory already accepts.
2. **Export range plan as XLSX** — aimily's range plan accepts an XLSX import with column mapping; the platform back-fills missing fields by inference from your imported brand DNA.
3. **Recreate active SKUs** — aimily auto-suggests materials from its 963-entry library based on your historic BOMs.
4. **Re-onboard the team** in 1-2 weeks; the UI is meaningfully simpler than Centric's because aimily isn't trying to be 800 brands' simultaneous customization layer.

For a hands-on migration walk-through: `hello@aimily.app`. We've done several from Centric and from FlexPLM.
